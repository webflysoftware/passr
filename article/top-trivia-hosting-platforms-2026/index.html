Top Trivia Hosting Platforms of 2026

We needed a pub quiz for the team offsite. Somehow that turned into ranking half the internet.

Priya Sharma Jun 11, 2026 6 min read

Every company runs a trivia night eventually. HR sends the calendar invite. Someone gets volunteered to host. Then comes the real question: which platform are we using, and will it work when Dave from Finance joins on a flaky hotel WiFi connection?

We ranked five platforms for real team events — hosted rooms, flaky WiFi, and someone who volunteered reluctantly. None are perfect. Here is the list, best to worst.

RoomSignal

The newest name here. Browser-based audience engagement: polls, quizzes, pulse checks, decision trees, and a growing pile of interaction types. Think TriviaRat meets Mentimeter — no apps, no logins, but with word clouds and pulse checks when trivia is only part of the night.

Pros

  • Lots of games and real-time interactions beyond trivia
  • Fairly inexpensive compared to the enterprise crowd
  • Browser-only setup keeps event-day support tickets low

Cons

  • Trivia is one feature among many, so quiz workflow is shallower than a dedicated pub quiz tool

TriviaRat

The classic pub-style trivia platform — probably the closest you get to pen and paper. Lightweight, no apps, no logins, and an experience most players forget they used. If you do not need email registration, tournaments, or side games, this is the one. Free-form text entry instead of four colorful buttons.

Pros

  • Dead simple to set up; the host controls pacing between play, mark, and leaderboard
  • Probably the cheapest option here, with a usable free tier
  • Free-form answers instead of multiple choice

Cons

  • Trivia only — no spin-the-wheel side quests or word clouds
  • Built for in-room events; 500+ remote players can lag system a bit
  • No multi-language support

Kahoot

If you went to school after 2015, you know Kahoot. Buzzers, colors, that music. A game-based learning platform that escaped the classroom and ended up at every company social held on Zoom.

Pros

  • Tons of templates!
  • Integrations with SSO and other services
  • Customer support is responsive and helpful

Cons

  • Pricing creeps up as you add features
  • Built for classrooms, not pubs/corporate events — weak free-form answer marking

Mentimeter

What happens when a presentation tool and an audience response system have a very productive meeting. Polls, quizzes, Q&A, word clouds, real-time feedback — plus PowerPoint and Teams integration for the person who lives in slide decks.

Pros

  • Build presentations inside it; customize leaderboards
  • Slides in, engagement out — good for town halls with a trivia round

Cons

  • The free plan is tight — you may hit the limit mid-event
  • General-purpose engagement, not trivia-first

CrowdPurr

The corporate engagement Swiss Army knife. Live, virtual, hybrid, team-building — events where someone says "synergy" unironically. You have seen it at conferences. Your manager has bookmarked it.

Pros

  • Handles large crowds and deep customization, including Excel and Microsoft integrations
  • Strong fit when legal, IT, and procurement all have opinions

Cons

  • Getting started feels like onboarding to enterprise software, because it basically is
  • Pricing scales with device count — corporate for "this gets expensive fast"

So which one?

  • Top pick for covering tonsn of engagement types: RoomSignal
  • Best overall for classic trivia, simplicity, and affordability: TriviaRat
  • Fast setup, lots of customizations: Kahoot
  • Trivia inside a slide deck or town hall: Mentimeter
  • 800-person corporate all-hands with a budget: CrowdPurr

Pick the tool that matches your room size, your host's patience, and how much you like reading pricing pages. Everything else is marketing.
