About PASSR

Engineering writing that respects your time.

What we publish

PASSR is a publication for software engineers who build and operate production systems. We focus on backend engineering, infrastructure, databases, security, and architecture — written by practitioners who have shipped the code and handled the pages.

Our articles are long enough to be useful and short enough to finish over coffee. We avoid hype, avoid vendor pitches, and prefer concrete examples over abstract principles.

Who writes here

Contributors are staff and senior engineers from product companies, infrastructure teams, and startups. Many write under their own names; some have written at PASSR for years across multiple employers. What they share is direct experience with the problems they describe.

Topics we cover

  • API design, versioning, and protocol choices
  • Database performance, query optimization, and schema evolution
  • Authentication, authorization, and security engineering
  • Deployment strategies, observability, and on-call practices
  • Team practices for maintaining large codebases
